SLIDE 2 — Concept: What Makes a Sentence Complete?
---------------------------------------------------
What makes a sentence complete?

Every sentence needs:

SUBJECT → Who or what
PREDICATE → What they do or are

Example:
"Kwame Nkrumah [SUBJECT]
 led Ghana to independence. [PREDICATE]"


SLIDE 3 — Passage: Ghana's Independence (With Problem Sentences)
----------------------------------------------------------------
📄 Read this passage carefully:

"Ghana gained independence on the 6th of March, 1957.
 Declared by Kwame Nkrumah at the Old Polo Grounds in Accra.
 The people were very.
 Ghana was the first country in sub-Saharan Africa
 independent became from colonial rule by the British
 who had controlled it for many years before then."

⚠ Can you spot the problems?

--- HIGHLIGHTS (shown in amber) ---
1. "Declared by Kwame Nkrumah at the Old Polo Grounds in Accra."
   → Missing subject. Incomplete sentence.

2. "The people were very."
   → Incomplete predicate. Sentence cuts off.

3. "independent became from colonial rule by the British
    who had controlled it for many years before then."
   → Incoherent, overloaded structure. Meaning is lost.


SLIDE 4 — Corrections: Let's Fix It Together
---------------------------------------------
✏ Let's fix it together

✗ "Declared by Kwame Nkrumah at the Old Polo Grounds."
✓ "Kwame Nkrumah declared independence at the
    Old Polo Grounds in Accra."

✗ "The people were very."
✓ "The people were very joyful."

✗ Overloaded, jumbled sentence
✓ "Ghana became the first independent country in
    sub-Saharan Africa, ending years of British
    colonial rule."
